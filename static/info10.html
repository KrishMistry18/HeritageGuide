Government Museum (Egmore)

Photos

About

Established in 1851, the Government Museum in Egmore is the second-oldest museum in India and one of the most comprehensive repositories of South Indian art, archaeology, and history. The museum houses an extensive collection of bronze sculptures, with the most notable being the Chola-era Nataraja statue, a masterpiece of ancient Indian craftsmanship.

The Numismatics Section showcases a vast collection of ancient coins, while the Archaeology Section preserves rare artifacts from the Pallava, Chola, and Vijayanagara dynasties. A major attraction is the Egyptian Gallery, featuring a well-preserved mummy, which is a rare sight in Indian museums.
