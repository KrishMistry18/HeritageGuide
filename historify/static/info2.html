Bandra Fort (Castella de Aguada)

Photos

About

Castella de Aguada, commonly known as Bandra Fort, is a historic Portuguese fort located at Land's End in Bandra, Mumbai. The name Castella de Aguada is a mispronunciation of the Portuguese term "Castelo da Aguada," which translates to "Fort of the Waterpoint." This name likely originates from the fresh water springs that were once present in the area, which provided a crucial water supply to passing ships. Although historical records indicate that the Portuguese originally referred to it as "Forte de Bandorá" (Bandra Fort), the present name has persisted over time.

Built in 1640, this strategic watchtower was designed to overlook Mahim Bay, the Arabian Sea, and the southern island of Mahim. Its primary function was to serve as a defensive outpost, guarding against naval invasions and securing Portuguese territories in Mumbai from attacks by rivals such as the Marathas and the British.

The fort was part of a larger network of coastal defenses constructed by the Portuguese in the region, complementing other forts like Vasai Fort and Madh Fort. However, as colonial power shifted, the British, after gaining control of the region in 1661, partially demolished the fort in 1739 to prevent it from being used against them by the advancing Maratha Empire.
